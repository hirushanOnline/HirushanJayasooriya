Trending Cybersecurity Attacks In 2020

02 June 2020 / Author: Hirushan Jayasooriya

What is a Cyber Attack ?

It is a well-planned, organized and intentionally launched activity chain launched by well-organized cybercriminals, targeting a one or more computing device to steal data, maliciously corrupt or disable devices, and gain access to unauthorized personnel data branch. Sometimes those attacks can be randomized or targeted. Those well-organized criminals use tons of different methods to launch their attacks in real time. With the rise of the globalization and emerging technologies of internet of things, cyber criminals are heading up by launching several types of attacks. Following are top 10 trending cybersecurity attacks in 2020.

Malware Attacks

The term malware stands to a code that is programmed to steal data, damage, or make changes to files and totally compromised a computer without the knowledge of the user. Malware can still remain inside a computer undetectable after infected to it. Malware is spreading across a computer network, and it can reduce network’s performance to a critical level. There are few types of malwares.

Trojan Horses

Trojan horse is a one type of malware which represent as a useful or legitimate software, but it does not. Cyber criminals use social engineering tips to inject those trojan horses into users’ systems and execute them within seconds. Once executed, Trojan Horses can be used to steal users’ data, spy on your activities, collect your behavior on the internet. Those Trojan horses can delete, block, modify, copy data in affected computer systems.

Ransomware

Most trending malware attack in recent few years. Ransomwares disrupt authorized access to a victim’s data or a computer system. Cyber criminals are threatening to victims to pay money as a ransom within a given limited time, if not they are deleting files. Most of the times criminals are requested to pay using cryptocurrencies. But there is no guarantee that victims can recover their files back even if they pay for the ransom. Ransomwares spread over a network with the support of Trojan Horses. Downloading pirated software leads to download a ransomware free of charge.

SQL Injection

Those type of attacks inject a maliciously programmed code to databases backend to retrieve information which are not supposed to display for the front end users. Those backend details include employee lists, financial statuses, private and confidential data of vendors. SQL Injection can lead to data corruption, unauthorized access to administrative database, deletion of important data tables in database and also unauthorized access to reveal sensitive information. Any kind of SQL based database can be a victim of SQL Injection attacks. These types of attacks can lead to loss of clients trust in the organizational unit if the users’ sensitive information got published without prior notice to them.

Artificial Intelligence powered Attacks

Concept of feeding and learning by its own to build and gather knowledge is mainly empowered by machine learning and big data analysis is becoming an emerging technology of modern computing systems. Those technologies are empowered in daily computing applications while its’ algorithms are processed using machine learning concepts. However Same set of systems can easily use to feed and learn to perform malicious attacks by its’ own. Artificial Intelligence powered attacks are trained to perform attacks repeatedly successfully suppressing obstacles.

Digital freedom stops where that of users begins... Nowadays, digital evolution must no longer be offered to a customer in trade-off between privacy and security. Privacy is not for sale, it's a valuable asset to protect.

SECURE YOUR MOBILE DEVICE FROM MALWARES

18 July 2020 / Author: Hirushan Jayasooriya

How mobile devices get affected ?

Downloading Third party Applications

Most of the cyber criminals use apps and downloads to spread malwares to users. Generally, the mobile applications you are getting through Google Play Store or Apple App Store are safe and guaranteed to be secured. But the apps that you are downloading from pirated or third party websites are not secured and most chances they are binded with malwares or spywares. Those applications looks legitimate, useful, and free to use. But it does not. Rarely an app with malicious code can be found in official App Store. In some countries, software developers also use pirated or cracked development tools for their development. Executions of applications from those developing tools contains malicious code to act as data stealing or damage mobile devices internal operability. Those kinds of applications are regularly monitored, and actions are taken to remove from the official store. Think twice when you are downloading applications rather than from the official stores by putting yourself into danger.

Phishing Text Messages

Sometimes You may receive text messages which requests to submit your personal sensitive information through text messages. Sometimes it can be a link to a form to submit your data. It may looks legitimate. But always it does not. Cyber criminals use these data to access and breach into what they can access. Specially providing personal information like social security numbers, credit card or financial details can be really dangerous. They may be use the same kind of malware spreading techniques to perform a well planned attack and install it to mobile device without the consent of yours. If you have received a text message or a link like this, be motivated to immediately contact the relevant company named in the text message through a legitimate contact method and get verified that if they has really send you such a message.

Accessing suspicious emails

Sometimes you may receive an email to your corporate email account saying your official cloud storage is under attack. Click here to take immediate actions now. Or saying you have won a lottery or a mobile device or anything related that. And click here to claim your gift. Those emails appears as legitimate and if you open and click the provided link, it takes you to a dummy AdWords site or redirect to your homepage. Simply it does nothing as it mentioned in the email. But in the backend of the system, you have given the permission to activate the malware binded with the link to act according to its programmed malicious nature. The data inside your mobile device is exposed and compromised to the cyber criminal who has planed the attack.

Time to protect your Mobile Device
  • As every aspects of mobile devices are facing nature to cyber attacks and related threats, still there are ways to protect it avoid getting victimized. Here are some tips to secure mobile devices from user perspective.

  • Do not root and install third party custom operating systems into mobile devices. Most of the readily available security features will cancelled and the device becomes easier to compromised.

  • You can use Virtual Private Networks (VPNs) to share information over a public Wi-Fi networks without getting compromised to attackers.

  • Download mobile applications only from the trusted sources, since third party applications and hosted sites may be suspected of cyber attackers.

  • If you think you have important data files inside your mobile application, make sure to use an encryption method to secure your files without losing access to them.

  • Make sure to update and all of the system and application software to the latest releases to facilitate the security patches done by the service providers.

  • If employees in an organization are using their mobile devices to maintain their office works, make sure to train them to avoid such malware and related attacks.

SECURE YOUR ONLINE PERSONAL IDENTITY

28 october 2020 / Author: Hirushan Jayasooriya

Keep your personal information secure is an effective method to protect your online identity in the tremendously changing cyber space. During past few months while the Covid 19 pandemic situation is growing massively, a lot of security breaches, cyber threats, cyber bullying, Data dissemination and many more issues related to data breaches and vulnerabilities were identified. We are going to briefly identify few methods to how to keep your personal information secure to protect your online identity while surfing the cyber space.

Keep an eye on Fraudsters

Identify in the first place whom you are sharing your personal information with. Personal and financial information of a person is more sensitive than any data. Make sure you do not share your personal information with third parties without knowing the proper digital signature of them. If someone sends you an email attaching some links to forms or any kind of data collecting methods, do not click those links at once trusting them in first place. Contact the vendor by searching their name in web and find their websites. Try to contact them via contact numbers provided on the website to check that are they really sent those data collection form. Make sure you are exposing your personal details for relevant and essentials parties only.

Avoid Oversharing On Social Networks

People used to share each and every moment of life on social medias as it becomes a part of lives. But maybe you have no idea about how that information can be important to identity thieves and they might use that identity information in guilty ways. Be careful to adjust your privacy settings of social media sites along with the time as the vendors update their services and privacy policies.

Tighten Your Passwords

It is totally normal for people to forget what they have set up as passwords for most of their accounts. Because of that reason, they try to set up easy passwords to remember. Most of them are using personal details as passwords like birthdays, special dates, surnames and etc. Those type of passwords are easy to breach and can be easily guessed. Use strong and complex passwords by being creative. Of course, you can use your personal details. But use that in a smart way by substituting a part of it, not the whole details. Combine with special characters. Using a password manager are trending scenario to manage passwords. But make sure those password managers are trusted ones or not.

Secure Your Mobile Phone

A Smartphone contains more than 70% of a person’s sensitive data inside that. Social media networks, email access, Bank details, frequently used app, personal photos and videos, access to cloud database, GPS tracking details are very few of them. That is why you need to set up a strong encryption method to secure your all in one information device from fraudsters. Simple pattern or a few digit passwords are not really enough. At least you have go for bio metric based protection system. Facial recognition, fingerprint, bio metric authentication are best upcoming solutions for them.
